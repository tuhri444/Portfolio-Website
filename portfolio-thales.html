Thesis at Thales

Project information

Description

The goal of the thesis was to research the effects realism has on the fun and immersion of a game. To apply the knowledge a prototype was built for a buoyancy system that could be used for the naval ships that the player gets to control in the game. I additionally implemented a realistic procedural skybox instead of the static gradient one that was used as a placeholder.

  • Time: 22 weeks
  • Team structure: 1 programmer
  • Game Engine: Unity Engine
  • Language: C#
  • Roles: Grahpics programmer, Systems programmer

Details

Learning process

The project might have been for Thales working inside a team, but as a graduate, I got the space to work on my thesis and do the research that I needed to conduct.

A lot was learned about graphics programming and the use of processing data with the GPU using compute shaders. Greatly increasing my optimization capabilities. Subsequently, the knowledge was also applied to my hobby project, creating a 'game of life' simulation run using a single texture instead of thousands of individual game objects.


Buoyancy and implementation

Having consumed a high amount of Sea of Thieves content has given me a great appreciation for the amount of work that went into that game. Through my adventure of creating an ocean and implementing buoyancy, I've come to realize that it takes teams to build a good-looking and feeling ocean.

Having done research into pre-existing solutions for both buoyancy and ocean shaders, I came to the realization that there are plenty of open-source implementation guides that can be stitched together to create what I needed.
